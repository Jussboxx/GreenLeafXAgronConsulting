Context

The Problem & Our Approach

Green Leaf CEA | 8 Farm Locations | 20 Greenhouse Units | 120 Experimental Micro plots | British Columbia
Crops: Tomatoes, Peppers, Cucumbers, Strawberries | Climate Zones: Coastal, Fraser Valley, Interior | 60.3 Total Greenhouse Acres
The Problem
  • Independent BC farmers cannot accurately estimate operating costs
  • Rising input costs, volatile energy prices, and climate variability constrain financial planning
  • Limited access to capital without documented evidence of cost control
  • Precision agriculture technology exists, but upfront capital requirements remained a barrier for most independent operators
Our Approach
  • Based dashboard layout on the important metrics relevant to farmers
  • Leveraged Green Leaf's 9-dataset experiment program spanning 120 microplots
  • Developed a three-tab interactive dashboard covering Overview, Operations, and Finances
  • Connected daily sensor alerts and management responses to end-of-season financial outcomes
  • Quantified the return on investment from precision technology to support lender conversations

Takeaways

What This Season Proved

🌱
Precision Farming Pays Off
  • 95% of plots profitable across all 8 farm locations
  • Reduced Pest treatment delivered the highest ROI at ~4x per dollar spent
  • Precision treatments outperform control across all 4 crop types
  • Quantified ROI builds the case for lenders and capital access
⚡
Fast Response Protects Yield
  • 7,115 alerts managed with a 0.16-day (under 4hr) response time
  • Early action kept stress from becoming crop loss
  • April response delay directly correlated with peak stress index
  • Fast response protected profit on 95% of plots season-wide
📊
Data Guides Next Season
  • Strawberry leads yield at 322 kg/mS — double down on wins
  • 4 plots (P0118, P0082, P0025, P0106) need priority care
  • Stress levels flag which crops need attention next season
  • Clear roadmap to cut losses and optimize planting decisions
